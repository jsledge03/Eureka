🔷 STRATEGIC INTENT

(Foundational. Declarative. Focused.)

Tooltip (ⓘ)

Strategic Intent clarifies what you are optimizing for right now.
It defines what matters most this season — and what you are willing to deprioritize.

It is not metrics.
It is not a to-do list.
It is a leadership stance.

When decisions compete, Strategic Intent decides.

⸻

Field Prompt (editable text area)

Prompt header:
What are you optimizing for in this season?

Helper text:
Complete the sentence:
	•	“Right now, I am prioritizing ___ over ___.”
	•	“For this season, success means ___.”
	•	“I am protecting ___ and intentionally letting go of ___.”
	•	“If nothing else moves forward, I will ensure ___.”

Encourage clarity over complexity.
One or two sentences is enough.

⸻

Example (placeholder, not pre-filled)

“Right now, I am prioritizing stability and energy recovery over aggressive expansion. I will protect sleep, fitness, and deep work consistency.”

⸻

🔷 STRATEGIC TRAJECTORY

(Directional. Reflective. Course-correcting.)

Tooltip (ⓘ)

Strategic Trajectory shows where your current behaviors are leading.

It analyzes:
	•	Alignment trends
	•	Friction patterns
	•	Drift signals
	•	Energy and load trends

It answers:
If you keep operating this way, what happens next?

It helps you steer — not react.

⸻

Trajectory Structure (Auto-Generated)

1) Direction Summary (2 sentences max)

Tone example:

“Your system is trending toward stable execution with rising load. Momentum is strong, but recovery signals are weakening.”

OR

“Your current operating pattern supports your stated intent. Drift risk is low, and domain balance is stabilizing.”

⸻

2) Tailwinds (What’s working)
	•	Consistent keystone habit execution
	•	Overdue backlog decreasing
	•	Reflection frequency rising
	•	Friction declining in high-priority domain

Language tone:

“Execution discipline is compounding.”

⸻

3) Headwinds (What’s costing you)
	•	Rising friction in Fitness
	•	Overdue tasks increasing midweek
	•	Reduced recovery signals
	•	Frequent overload check-ins

Tone:

“System strain is emerging in ___.”

No shame. No drama.

⸻

4) Course Corrections (Actionable, clickable)

Buttons:
	•	Reduce active goals to 3
	•	Convert ___ to Minimum Version
	•	Schedule 15-min backlog sprint
	•	Switch to Stabilize season
	•	Protect recovery block
	•	Add constraint: No new commitments this week

Each must apply real change.

⸻

🔷 WEEKLY EXECUTIVE MEMO

(Board-level self-review)

Header

Weekly Executive Memo
Week of: [Date Range]

⸻

Section 1: What You Protected

“You protected Deep Work and Fitness despite midweek friction.”

⸻

Section 2: What Drifted (and likely why)

“Reflection declined after Tuesday. Energy ratings dropped, suggesting load exceeded sustainable range.”

⸻

Section 3: Emerging Patterns

“Friction clusters around late evenings. Overdue tasks accumulate when task count exceeds 5.”

⸻

Section 4: Strategic Recommendations

Tone: decisive but calm.

	1.	Reduce active goals to 3 for clarity.
	2.	Protect one recovery block midweek.
	3.	Convert 1 habit to Minimum Version to maintain consistency.

Each recommendation must be actionable.

⸻

🔷 COACH VOICE REFINEMENT

(Across entire app)

Coach tone should feel:
	•	Strategic, not motivational
	•	Calm, not urgent
	•	Precise, not verbose
	•	Compassionate, not indulgent

Examples:

Instead of:

“You’re falling behind.”

Use:

“System load is rising. Simplifying now preserves momentum.”

Instead of:

“You failed to complete habits.”

Use:

“Consistency dipped. Would you like to redesign for sustainability?”

Instead of:

“You must reduce goals.”

Use:

“Reducing to 3 active goals may increase clarity and follow-through.”
