🔵 Daily Rhythm Tooltip Copy

Title:
Daily Rhythm

Body:

Your Daily Rhythm is not about perfection.
It’s about alignment, energy awareness, and consistent movement.

Each day moves through five stages:

State – Your energy and capacity.

Intent – What matters today.

Action – Habits and tasks completed.

Reflection – What worked and what didn’t.

Adaptation – Adjusting tomorrow accordingly.

Low-energy days are not failures.
Using a Minimum Version or reducing scope is intelligent self-leadership.

This is a compass — not a grade.

🟣 Identity Alignment Score Tooltip Copy

Title:
Identity Alignment

Body:

This score reflects how closely your actions matched the person you’re becoming.

It blends four signals:

• Intentionality (30%)
Did you define what mattered today?

• Congruent Action (40%)
Did your completed habits and tasks support your goals and identity?

• Integrity Under Constraints (20%)
On low-energy days, did you adapt wisely instead of forcing it?

• Reflection (10%)
Did you close the loop and learn?

This score is about congruence — not volume.
A focused, aligned day can score higher than a busy one.

This is feedback, not judgment.

🟢 Rhythm Score Tooltip Copy

Title:
Rhythm Score

Body:

Rhythm measures consistency over time — not streaks.

It uses a rolling 14-day window:

• Habits (70%) – Are you showing up for what you scheduled?
• Tasks (30%) – Are commitments being completed reliably?

Grace days count as neutral — they prevent shame spirals while protecting consistency.

Rhythm rewards steadiness, not intensity.

You don’t need perfect days.
You need sustainable ones.

🟡 Momentum Tooltip Copy (if shown)

Title:
Momentum

Body:

Momentum reflects forward movement in the present week.

It increases when:
• You complete priority tasks
• You execute keystone habits
• You follow through on your reset intentions

It dips when:
• Tasks accumulate without triage
• Habits are repeatedly skipped without adaptation

Momentum is directional — not moral.

If it dips, recalibrate.
If it rises, protect the structure that created it.
